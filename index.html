Sarpy County, NE Outdoor Warning Sirens

Prepared by Steve Zrnic

Maps

Overview

The goal of this site is to educate the residents and leaders of Sarpy County, NE about the current status of the outdoor warning sirens in town. While the system is relativelty up to date, there are still old models left behind from the previous civil defense system. Unlike the new sirens, these are powered by AC only. The new sirens have battery backup capabilties so in the event of a power outage, the siren can still operate and warn the public.

The map shows the older sirens currently left in the system. This is any model that isn't the currently used Federal Signal Corporation 2001-130 siren. The new sirens are louder than the older ones, and have battery backup which is a big plus. The 2001-130 is 130 decibels at 100 feet, and rotates 360 degrees sending sounds in all directions. The older sirens are primarily omni directional, and are about 100 to 115 decibels at 100 feet. In the event of a power outage, the old sirens will not activate.

As the maps show, the new sirens allow for less sirens needing to be installed to provide adequate coverage. Despite this, the county has replaced sirens one on one with the new model. While over coverage isn't a bad thing, the fact that each unit can range from $25,000 to $30,000 is a big expense. Going forward, it is strongly urged to use this map to determine whether or not the old sirens should be replaced, or should just be abandoned once they fail. In most cases, if an older siren fails, there would be zero impact as it is already covered by two or three newer sirens. This would ultimately save the county and taxpayers money without comprimising safety.
